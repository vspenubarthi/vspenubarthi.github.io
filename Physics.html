Physics

Here at Mass. Academy, we learn a fast-paced physics course that aligns with the AP Physics C exam. Our teacher, Mr. Ellis, strives to make sure that we do not just memorize the principals of physics, but that we understand the fundamentals and gain an appreciation for the amazing subject. Like many of the other courses in the school, this course also heavily incorporates labs and group projects to make sure that we can physically see the physics concepts that we are learning and learn to work in groups as well.

In Physics, we often work on Uber Problems, which are Physics problems that incorporate multiple Physics concepts and require a lot of time and effort to solve. After we find the solution and get it checked, we must write up our solution which carefully details the steps we took to get to our answer and usually incorporates a diagram and graphs that accompany the problems as well.

As mentioned above, we do a lot of labs in Physics to see many of the concepts that we learn about. Recently, we worked on a Spring Energy Lab, in which we had to come up with our own experiment that analyzed the conservation of energy in a spring-based system. As I am on the calculus Physics track, I, along with my groupmates, did an experiment which did this analysis utilizing a non-linear spring.
