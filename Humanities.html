Humanities

At Mass. Academy, we learn Humanities rather than a traditional English course. While we do read texts from different time periods and write analytical papers on them, our teacher, Ms. Small, tries to make sure that we are broadening our thinking as we read these texts so that we develop as intellectuals. One of the many fun things that we did this year was going on a trip to Walden Pond after reading Walden, where we got to see where Henry David Thoreau resided during his excursion. It was a great experience, and I would definitely recommend going there.

One of my favorite essays I wrote in Humanities was the Egyptian Secular Poem. For this assignment, we got to analyze poems written by tomb builders from ancient Egypt. Overall, these poems were incredibly amusing as a lot of them described love in a comical way. After that, we were allowed to pick a theme of our choice and write a synthesis paper on it using the poems that we had read. I wrote my paper about the many instances of comparing love to other things through the use of extended metaphors.

Another one of my favorite essays I wrote in this class was the Ancient Greek Essay. For this essay, we got to analyze The Iliad and The Women of Troy. These texts are both unique for their own reasons, as The Iliad is a legendary epic and The Women of Troy offers a unique perspective of the world after the events of the Iliad. After reading these two pieces, I wrote a paper detailing how the differences between the ideologies of the people in these time periods affected the views of the people in these writings.
