Volunteering

From a young age, my parents instilled the importance of giving back in me. As a result, I have been involved in volunteering on various levels throughout my childhood. A lot of my volunteering is related to engineering and robotics, two things that I am extremely passionate about. However, I also enjoy volunteering at Community Harvest Project, a farm that grows food and donates it to people in need, as well.

During the summer of my junior year, I volunteered at a Summer Lego Camp for kids as part of FRC Team 467. I was a captain during the program and led my team of 6 middle-school kids through various activities including building forts on a budget, making rockets, building solar cars, and creating Lego robots that would compete in competitions.

Lego Coaching

In addition to all the fun volunteering and activities over the summer, I also coach my own Lego Robotics team. I think that the best part of this entire experience was how much I got to see the game and the kids learn that all hard work might not payoff. I think that for students in middle school, the six boys that I coach were poised and calm in the heat of the competition. This year, they improved so much that they won the Champion’s Award for the top team in the Shrewsbury competition and they also won a first-place engineering design award at the regional level. Nearby, there is also a photo taken from one of the demonstrations our team did this year. Each year, the team demonstrates their robot and their engineering skills for elementary school students so that they can be amazed and get interested and hopefully build robots themselves. I see a bright future for them and the team and I will continue coaching, and if you are an adult or a student who wants to coach and wants tips to start, contact me with the email below.
